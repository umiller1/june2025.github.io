One Pot Pumpkin Mac & Chez

Pumpkin season calls for hearty, comforting recipes, and this one-pot mac and cheese is exactly that. Don’t worry, it doesn’t taste like a pumpkin spice latte. The pumpkin adds a natural sweetness, just like tomatoes or carrots would in a sauce. The result is a rich, velvety mac and cheese with a subtle fall twist that feels both warming and familiar.

Ingredients:

  • 12 ounces pasta, shape of choice
  • 3 tablespoons unsalted butter
  • 1 tablespoon minced garlic
  • 5 sage leaves
  • 2 sprigs thyme
  • 4 cups chicken broth
  • 1 cup pumpkin purée
  • ⅓ cup heavy cream
  • 1 teaspoon dry mustard powder
  • ¼ teaspoon onion powder
  • 1 teaspoon smoked paprika
  • ½ teaspoon salt, adjust to taste
  • ⅛ teaspoon nutmeg, more to taste
  • dash of cayenne powder, to taste
  • 1 cup sharp cheddar cheese, shredded
  • ½ cup gouda, shredded
  • ½ cup colby jack, shredded
  • parmesan cheese, grated (for garnish)
  • black pepper, freshly cracked (for garnish)

Instructions:

  1. In a large pot over medium heat, melt the butter. Add the garlic, sage, and thyme and cook until fragrant, about 2 to 3 minutes. Remove the sage leaves and thyme sprigs and discard them.
  2. Still over medium heat, pour in the chicken broth and stir in the pumpkin purée and heavy cream until smooth. Season with mustard powder , onion powder, cayenne, smoked paprika, salt, and nutmeg.
  3. Raise the heat to medium-high, add the pasta, and bring the mixture to a boil. Reduce to a simmer and cook uncovered, stirring frequently, until the pasta is tender and most of the liquid has been absorbed, about 14 to 15 minutes.
  4. Lower the heat to the lowest setting, add the cheeses, and gently fold them in with a rubber spatula until completely melted into the sauce. Taste and adjust seasoning as needed.
  5. Serve hot, garnished with parmesan and freshly cracked black pepper.

Matchert Milkt Jamt

This matcha milk jam is thick, creamy, and packed with deep matcha flavor in every spoonful. It slowly cooks down until rich and jammy, then gets whisked with vibrant matcha for the perfect earthy finish. Spread it on toast, drizzle it over pancakes, or just eat it straight from the jar.

Ingredients:

  • 1 1/4 cups whole milk
  • 1 1/4 cups heavy cream
  • 1/2 cup granulated sugar
  • 2 teaspoons cornstarch
  • 1/4 cup milk powder
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1 tablespoon matcha powder
  • 2 tablespoons room temp water

Instructions:

  1. In a medium saucepan, whisk together the milk, cream, sugar, cornstarch, milk powder, and vanilla extract until smooth and fully dissolved.
  2. Place the pan over medium-low heat and cook, stirring often, until it begins to gently bubble.
  3. Continue cooking on low to medium-low heat for 45 minutes to 1 hour, stirring frequently to prevent sticking. The mixture should reduce and thicken to a creamy, jammy consistency that coats the back of a spoon.
  4. While it’s cooking, whisk the matcha powder with the water in a small bowl using a W and M motion until completely smooth and lump-free.
  5. Once the milk jam has thickened, remove the pan from heat. Add the matcha mixture and whisk until fully combined and smooth.
  6. Pour the matcha milk jam into a clean jar and let it cool to room temperature. It will thicken slightly as it cools.
  7. Store in the refrigerator for up to one week. It continues to firm up the longer it sits in the fridge, so give it a stir before using if needed!

Pão de Deus

Pão de Deus translates to "Bread of God," and after one bite, you’ll understand why. I had it in Portugal and it was heavenly—sweet, soft, and topped with the most luscious coconut crust. Honestly... dare I say, it might be even better than a pastel de nata.

Ingredients:

  • 1 (12-pack) original Hawaiian rolls
  • 1/2 cup finely grated coconut
  • 1/4 cup granulated sugar
  • 1 large egg
  • 1 egg yolk
  • 1/4 cup unsalted butter, melted
  • 1/4 cup dry milk powder
  • 1/4 cup cake flour
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • powdered sugar, for topping

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C). Line a baking sheet with parchment paper.
  2. Place the whole dozen pack of Hawaiian rolls on the baking sheet, keeping them connected.
  3. In a bowl, whisk together the grated coconut, granulated sugar, large egg, egg yolk, melted butter, dry milk powder, cake flour, and vanilla extract until a thick, cohesive paste forms.
  4. Spoon the coconut topping evenly over the tops of each roll.
  5. Spoon the coconut topping evenly over the tops of each roll.
  6. Remove from the oven and allow the rolls to cool for 10 minutes.
  7. Dust generously with powdered sugar before serving.
